Nasi Lemak

Nasi Lemak is often regarded as Malaysia's national dish. It features fragrant rice cooked in rich coconut milk, imparting a creamy texture and subtle sweetness. Traditionally served on a banana leaf, Nasi Lemak is accompanied by a variety of sides, including spicy sambal, crispy fried anchovies, roasted peanuts, hard-boiled or fried eggs, and fresh cucumber slices, creating a harmonious blend of flavors and textures.




Recipe Details
Portion 2-3 servings
Preparation Time 20 minutes
Cooking Time 30 minutes
Difficulty Medium
Cuisine Malaysian
Taste Profile Rich, creamy, and slightly spicy
Serving Suggestions Best served hot with sambal, eggs, peanuts, and cucumber
Nutrition Facts (per serving)
Calories 450 kcal
Fat 18g
Saturated Fat 12g
Carbohydrates 50g
Sugar 4g
Protein 15g
Sodium 500mg

Ingredients Measurement
Cooking oil 2 tbsp
Shallots, finely sliced 1
Ginger, julienned 1 inch
Garlic, minced 1 clove
Rice, rinsed 500 g
Coconut milk 200 ml
Pandan leaves, knotted 2 leaves
Red onion, sliced for sambal 1
MAGGI Sambal Tumis Paste 2 tbsp or as desired
Anchovies, fried until crispy 1.5 cups
Tamarind paste, diluted 0.25 tsp
Water 100 ml
Hard-boiled eggs, halved 3
Cucumber, sliced 1
Peanuts, toasted 1 cup
MAGGI Cukup Rasa 1 tbsp

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Sauté shallots, ginger, and garlic until fragrant and lightly golden.
  2. Add the rinsed rice to the pan and stir briefly to coat the grains in the aromatics. Transfer the mixture to a rice cooker.
  3. Add coconut milk, water, MAGGI Cukup Rasa, and knotted pandan leaves to the rice cooker. Stir well, then cook until the rice is fluffy and fragrant.
  4. For the sambal, heat a separate pan with oil. Sauté the sliced red onion until soft, then add MAGGI Sambal Tumis Paste. Stir well and let it simmer for 3-5 minutes.
  5. Add water, fried anchovies, tamarind paste, and a small amount of sugar to balance the flavors. Simmer until the sambal thickens, about 5-7 minutes.
  6. To serve, spoon the cooked rice onto a plate or banana leaf. Add sambal, hard-boiled eggs, cucumber slices, toasted peanuts, and extra fried anchovies as garnishes.
