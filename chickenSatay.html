Chicken Satay

Chicken Satay is a popular dish in Malaysian cuisine, featuring marinated chicken skewers that are grilled to perfection. Typically served with a rich peanut sauce, this dish offers a delightful balance of savory and sweet flavors, making it a favorite for both locals and visitors.




Recipe Details
Portion 4 servings
Preparation Time 30 minutes
Cooking Time 15 minutes
Difficulty Medium
Cuisine Malaysian
Taste Profile Savory and slightly sweet
Serving Suggestions Best served with peanut sauce for dipping
Nutrition Facts (per serving)
Calories 400 kcal
Fat 22g
Saturated Fat 8g
Carbohydrates 10g
Sugar 4g
Protein 35g
Sodium 600mg

Ingredients Measurement
Boneless chicken thighs, cut into cubes 500 g
Curry powder 2 tsp
Red curry paste 1 tbsp
Coconut milk 1/2 cup
Sugar 1 tbsp
Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the chicken pieces with curry powder, red curry paste, coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Mix well and marinate for at least 20 minutes or up to 2 hours in the refrigerator.
  2. Preheat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 10 minutes to prevent burning.
  3. Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto skewers, leaving a bit of space between each piece.
  4. Grill the skewers for 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is fully cooked and has a nice char. Brush with extra marinade as needed.
  5. To make the peanut sauce, mix peanut butter, coconut milk, red curry paste, soy sauce, sugar, and vinegar in a saucepan over low heat. Stir until smooth and heated through.
  6. Serve the grilled chicken satay hot with peanut sauce on the side for dipping.
